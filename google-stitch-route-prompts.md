# Google Stitch Route Prompts

Use these prompts to explore individual pages or sections after the main homepage exploration.

All prompts below assume the same shared constraints:

- responsive web design
- no backend/auth/API/admin dashboard references
- font weights limited to 400 and 700
- type scale capped at 5 sizes total
- body line-height 1.6
- heading line-height 1.1
- color palette limited to 1 background color, 1 text color, and 1 accent color
- avoid generic SaaS UI, dashboard UI, and default developer-portfolio aesthetics
- prioritize layout, typography, composition, cropping, and rhythm over decorative color complexity
- the final site should balance minimal/editorial clarity with expressive, art-directed presentation

## 1. Projects Index

```text
Design a responsive Projects index page for Yuxing Zhou's personal website.

Context:
- This is a personal site focused on personal branding, creative expression, and strong presentation of public work.
- Projects are one of the two most identity-defining pillars, along with Writing.
- This page should help visitors quickly scan selected projects and decide which ones to open.

Structure:
- Include a clear page title and short framing copy.
- Present a curated collection of project entries.
- Each project should appear as:
  - a thumbnail or small visual
  - a title
  - a short summary
  - optional metadata such as year, role, or type
- The overall page should feel structured and browsable, not dense.
- Avoid a resume-style wall of text.
- The page should support both visual scanning and credibility.

Aesthetic:
- Editorial and intelligent, but not stiff.
- More structured than the photography or AI-generated media pages.
- Keep media present, but secondary to clarity and hierarchy.

Design goals:
- Make projects feel serious, memorable, and easy to compare.
- Support a future mix of product, engineering, research, or creative projects.
- Leave room for some projects to feel more prominent than others without turning the page into a rigid list.

Generate multiple concepts that vary in:
- card layout versus list layout
- amount of thumbnail prominence
- how metadata is handled
- how much whitespace versus density is used
```
